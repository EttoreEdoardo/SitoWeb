GOAL 10 - ITALIAN SITUATION

Italy and Goal 10

Contrast to income poverty

The PNRR foresees that 40% of the resources are invested in the South (about 82 billion).
The measures most likely to have a significant positive impact on southern incomes in the short term are:

  • the enhancement of the ultra-broadband, which will allow the digitization of local authorities and businesses, and authorize access to the tax benefits of the Transition Plan 4.0;
  • the strengthening of the Special Economic Zones.

    • In the medium term, the greatest benefits for the South will come from the strengthening of the infrastructural network. In terms of active employment policies, the NRP allocates 0.6 billion for the strengthening of employment centers, including staff training.

    Support for disabled, elderly or vulnerable people

    The pandemic has accelerated the process of reforming home care for insufficient elderly people, an issue that is destined to become increasingly relevant taking into account the rapid aging of the population. A journey that began with the allocation of additional resources, initially by the Relaunch Decree (DL 19 May 2020) and subsequently by the PNRR. There will be four main points of the reform in support of non self-sufficient elderly that is taking shape:

  • a system oriented on the primacy of support for home care and, more generally, towards the implementation of local and neighborhood health;
  • the increase in the offer of services;
  • the strengthening of intervention models following a multidimensional care model, with the integration in particular of social assistance health interventions, also taking into account the specific and different conditions of the elderly and their families;
  • the improvement of the multilevel coordination system and the simplification of access routes.

    • Equal opportunities and anti-discrimination policies

    With the Legislative Decree of 8 June 2021, the Government established the single and universal allowance for families (with simultaneous abolition of personal income tax deductions for dependent children), thanks to which the number of potentially beneficiary families increases by 1.8 million compared to to the pre-existing measures. Under the current bridge decree, the benefits:

  • they are accessible to families with less than 50 thousand euros of ISEE;
  • decrease as family income increases;
  • they increase as the number of children increases;
  • they concern both self-employed and dependent workers.

    • The PNRR also allocates 4.6 billion to the upgrading of nursery schools and childcare services, envisaging an increase of 230 thousand available places, to be added to the current 355 thousand.

    Immigration

    The main novelties of the delegated law proposal on the tax reform presented to the Chambers in June are:

  • the lowering of the effective average rate for taxpayers with income between 28 thousand and 55 thousand euros;
  • the abolition of IRAP;
  • the reduction of the rate on financial income and its alignment with the first rate of personal income tax;
  • la riduzione dell’aliquota dell’IVA ordinaria;
  • the extension of the flat tax for the self-employed.

    • Improvement of competition and institutions

    The PNRR incorporates many of the recommendations from the Italian Antitrust Authority regarding the strengthening of competition in various sectors, from the development of infrastructures, to tenders for the provision of public services or the management of public goods (for example, the strengthening of monitoring in the context of in-house assignments for the management of local public transport, greater transparency within the accreditation system of private health facilities, or the strengthening of tenders for motorway concessions), up to the removal of barriers entering the energy sector markets. Considering the very delicate period we are experiencing, in which, alongside the uncertainties due to the pandemic, those relating to the conflict between Russia and Ukraine have been added, with its impact on all the economies of the world (without the exception of our own), the provisions of the PNRR it is a true light of hope for the perspectives of the individual and of the entire community.
